When to Plant Vegetables: Why Your Zip Code Matters More Than the Date

July 5, 2026

Generic calendars say "plant tomatoes in April." But April in Florida is not April in Minnesota. The single most important factor is your local frost dates.

Why Zip Code Matters

USDA zones are broad. Within Zone 7, microclimate can shift dates by 2-3 weeks. PlantingCalendar uses your zip code for personalized schedules.

3 Types of Vegetables

Cool-season (lettuce, peas, spinach): plant 4-6 weeks before last frost. Warm-season (tomatoes, peppers): plant after last frost. Succession crops (beans, radishes): plant every 2-3 weeks.

Start Indoors

Tomatoes and peppers need 6-8 weeks indoors before transplanting. Your calendar should include indoor start dates.
